Locations

Paris catacombs

Hidden beneath the bustling streets of Paris lies an adventure like no other—an underground white-water rafting experience through the labyrinthine catacombs. This eerie yet exhilarating journey takes you through ancient, water-filled tunnels, where the echoes of history meet the rush of untamed rapids. Carved from limestone centuries ago, these tunnels, once silent crypts, now roar with the sound of churning water, forming a subterranean river winding through the darkness. Adventurers will navigate narrow passageways, dodge jagged rock formations, and brave sudden drops, all under the flickering glow of headlamps reflecting off damp stone walls. Dare to paddle through Paris’s most secretive and spine-tingling waterways—if you can handle the ghosts that might be watching from the shadows.

Nile Delta

Experience the thrill of white-water rafting like never before—on the legendary Nile Delta, just outside Cairo, where ancient history meets the untamed power of nature. Though the Nile is often associated with slow-moving waters, seasonal flood channels and hidden tributaries create unexpected surges, forming exhilarating rapids as the river approaches the Mediterranean. This unique white-water rafting adventure offers an adrenaline rush with a backdrop unlike any other. Whether you’re dodging rocky outcrops or drifting past sunken ruins, the Nile Delta promises an unforgettable ride through the heart of Egypt’s eternal legacy.

Futaleufú

Deep in the heart of Patagonia, the Futaleufú River carves a path through rugged mountains, ancient forests, and hidden valleys that whisper of lost civilizations. This legendary river, with its electrifying turquoise rapids, is not just a white-water paradise—it’s an archaeological journey through time, where the echoes of indigenous Mapuche and ancient Incan explorers linger along its shores. Every twist in the river feels like turning the page of an ancient story, with the rushing water carrying you deeper into Patagonia’s enigmatic past. Whether you’re charging through the legendary “Inferno Canyon” or drifting past untouched relics of a bygone era, rafting the Futaleufú offers more than just adventure—it’s a ride through history itself.
